About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Finance at Universiti Tunku Abdul Rahman (UTAR), Malaysia, with an interdisciplinary research profile spanning financial economics, environmental governance, and computational engineering. My primary work investigates how ESG ratings, greenwashing behaviours, and media attention interact with corporate profitability in global firms. A parallel research agenda focuses on data-driven stochastic model predictive control for coordinating air pollution mitigation with carbon-neutral energy system optimisation. I also contribute to biomedical research through collaborative projects on retinal disease and neuroinflammation.
